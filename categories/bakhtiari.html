About Bakhtiari Rugs

Bakhtiari rugs are among the most durable and distinctive Persian tribal carpets, celebrated for their bold geometric designs, exceptional wool quality, and rich cultural heritage.

The History of Bakhtiari Rugs

The Bakhtiari people are one of Iran's most prominent tribal groups, inhabiting the mountainous regions of Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari Province. For generations, Bakhtiari women have woven rugs that reflect their environment, traditions, and tribal identity. These carpets gained international recognition during the nineteenth and twentieth centuries due to their durability and unique artistic character.

Distinctive Design Features

Bakhtiari rugs are famous for their bold tribal aesthetics and garden-inspired compositions.

  • Khesti Garden Panel designs
  • Large geometric medallions
  • Floral motifs
  • Tribal diamond patterns
  • Stylized birds and animals
  • Strong angular forms

Premium Materials and Craftsmanship

Authentic Bakhtiari rugs are hand-knotted using exceptionally strong mountain wool. Their dense construction makes them among the most durable Persian rugs ever produced.

  • Hand-spun wool
  • Natural vegetable dyes
  • Cotton foundations
  • Dense knotting techniques

Traditional Color Palette

Bakhtiari rugs often feature rich and vibrant colors.

  • Deep red
  • Navy blue
  • Ivory
  • Forest green
  • Brown
  • Gold accents

Why Choose a Bakhtiari Rug?

Bakhtiari rugs offer exceptional durability, authentic tribal character, rich Persian heritage, unique garden-inspired designs, and excellent long-term value. At Keshmiri Rugs, our Bakhtiari collection showcases the finest examples of this legendary Persian weaving tradition.
